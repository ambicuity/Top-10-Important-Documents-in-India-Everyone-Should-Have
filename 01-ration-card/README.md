# Ration Card

A ration card is a document issued by the state government to Indian nationals eligible to buy subsidized food grains, wheat, sugar, or kerosene from the Public Distribution System under the National Food Security Act (NFSA).

## What is a Ration Card?

A ration card is an official document that allows eligible Indian families to purchase essential commodities like food grains, wheat, sugar, and kerosene at subsidized rates from Fair Price Shops (ration shops) under the Public Distribution System.

## Why It Is Important

- **Proof of Residence**: Serves as a valid address proof for various purposes
- **Government Subsidies**: Access to subsidized food grains and essential commodities
- **Identity Document**: Used for Aadhaar and voter ID linkage
- **Family Record**: Contains details of all family members
- **Government Schemes**: Required for availing various state and central government schemes
- **School Admissions**: Often required for school admissions in government institutions

## Types of Ration Cards

1. **APL (Above Poverty Line)**: For families with annual income above poverty line
2. **BPL (Below Poverty Line)**: For families with annual income below poverty line
3. **AAY (Antyodaya Anna Yojana)**: For the poorest of the poor families
4. **Priority Household**: For families under National Food Security Act

## Eligibility

- Must be a bonafide resident of India
- Head of family must be above 18 years of age
- Family should not possess a ration card in any other state
- Minors and children below 18 are included in their family ration card

## Custom to Update Ration Card

When a new member comes into Indian families, like a baby, his/her name must be added to the member details of the ration card. It is a custom to add the name of a family member, new or old, to the ration card to buy their part of subsidised food.
