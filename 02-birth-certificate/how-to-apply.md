# How to Apply for Birth Certificate

## For Newborn Baby (Within 21 Days)

### Hospital Birth

**Step 1**: The hospital automatically registers the birth with the local municipal corporation or gram panchayat.

**Step 2**: Parents need to provide:
- Mother's identification documents
- Father's identification documents
- Hospital discharge summary
- Address proof

**Step 3**: Hospital issues a birth intimation slip within 24-48 hours.

**Step 4**: Visit the municipal corporation office or apply online on the state's birth certificate portal.

**Step 5**: Submit the hospital birth intimation slip and parent's documents.

**Step 6**: Receive the birth certificate (usually within 7-15 days).

**Note**: Registration within 21 days is **FREE** of cost.

### Home Birth

**Step 1**: Inform the local municipal corporation or gram panchayat within 21 days.

**Step 2**: Provide:
- Affidavit from parents on Rs. 10 stamp paper
- Proof of home delivery (midwife certificate, if any)
- Parent's identification documents
- Address proof
- Two witnesses with their identity proofs

**Step 3**: Fill the birth registration form.

**Step 4**: Submit at the registrar's office.

**Step 5**: Receive birth certificate after verification.

## Delayed Birth Certificate (After 21 Days to 1 Year)

**Step 1**: Visit the local municipal corporation or registrar's office.

**Step 2**: Submit:
- Application form for delayed registration
- Hospital birth record or discharge summary
- Affidavit from parents on Rs. 10 stamp paper
- Parent's identification documents
- Late fee (varies by state, usually ₹5-₹10)

**Step 3**: Verification by the registrar.

**Step 4**: Receive birth certificate within 15-30 days.

## Late Registration (After 1 Year)

**Step 1**: Apply to the registrar's office with a written application.

**Step 2**: Submit:
- School leaving certificate (if applicable)
- Immunization card
- Horoscope or baptism certificate
- Affidavit from parents and two witnesses
- Any other age proof documents

**Step 3**: Pay late fee (varies by state, usually ₹10-₹50).

**Step 4**: Executive Magistrate's permission required.

**Step 5**: Verification process (may take 30-60 days).

**Step 6**: Receive birth certificate after approval.

## Online Application Process

Most states now offer online birth certificate services:

### Step 1: Visit State Portal
Visit your state's online civil registration portal.

### Step 2: Register/Login
- Create an account or login with existing credentials
- Use mobile number and email for registration

### Step 3: Fill Application Form
- Select "Apply for Birth Certificate"
- Fill in all required details:
  - Child's name and gender
  - Date, time, and place of birth
  - Parent's details
  - Address information

### Step 4: Upload Documents
Upload scanned copies of:
- Hospital birth record/discharge summary
- Parent's ID proofs
- Address proof
- Marriage certificate (if applicable)

### Step 5: Pay Fee Online
- Pay the prescribed fee using credit/debit card, net banking, or UPI
- Save the payment receipt

### Step 6: Submit Application
- Review all details
- Submit the application
- Note down the application reference number

### Step 7: Track Status
- Use reference number to track application status
- Receive updates via SMS/email

### Step 8: Download/Collect Certificate
- Download e-certificate once approved
- Or collect physical certificate from designated office

## Documents Required

### For Normal Registration (Within 21 Days)
- Hospital discharge summary or birth intimation slip
- Parent's identification documents (Aadhaar/Voter ID/Passport)
- Parent's marriage certificate
- Address proof

### For Delayed Registration (21 Days to 1 Year)
- Hospital birth record
- Affidavit on Rs. 10 stamp paper
- Parent's ID proofs
- Address proof
- Late fee payment proof

### For Late Registration (After 1 Year)
- School admission record (if any)
- Immunization card
- Affidavit from parents and two witnesses
- Any available age proof
- Executive Magistrate's permission letter
- Late fee payment proof

## Fees Structure

| Registration Time | Fee |
|------------------|-----|
| Within 21 days | Free |
| 21 days to 1 year | ₹5-₹10 (varies by state) |
| After 1 year | ₹10-₹50 (varies by state) |
| Correction in certificate | ₹10-₹25 |
| Duplicate certificate | ₹15-₹50 |

**Note**: Fees may vary by state. Check your state portal for exact amounts.

## Processing Time

- **Within 21 days registration**: 7-15 days
- **Delayed registration**: 15-30 days
- **Late registration**: 30-60 days (requires magistrate approval)
- **Online application**: 10-20 days

## Corrections in Birth Certificate

If there are errors in the birth certificate:

1. Apply for correction at the issuing authority
2. Submit proof documents for the correction
3. Pay correction fee
4. Receive corrected certificate within 15-30 days

Common corrections:
- Name spelling
- Date of birth
- Parent's names
- Place of birth

## Important Tips

✅ Register birth within 21 days to avoid late fees
✅ Keep hospital discharge summary safe
✅ Get multiple copies of birth certificate
✅ Laminate the certificate to prevent damage
✅ Store digital copy in cloud storage
✅ Apply online for faster processing
