# Bank Passbook

Your bank passbook is a document that contains transaction details, like deposits and withdrawals with date, day, and year. Your bank passbook is a legal and top-important document in India that you need to get your pension money when you are eligible.

## What is a Bank Passbook?

A bank passbook is an official record book issued by banks to account holders that records all financial transactions made in the account. It serves as a ledger of deposits, withdrawals, interest earned, and account balance.

## Why You Need a Bank Passbook

### 1. Transaction Record
- Complete history of deposits and withdrawals
- Date-wise transaction details
- Running balance information
- Interest credited records

### 2. Address Proof
- Widely accepted as address proof
- Government-issued bank document
- Contains account holder details
- Valid for various applications

### 3. Identity Proof
- Account holder verification
- Contains photograph (in some banks)
- Signature specimen
- Valid identification document

### 4. Financial Statements
- Proof of financial stability
- Income verification
- Loan applications
- Visa applications

### 5. Pension Collection
- Essential for pension disbursement
- Proof of account for pension credit
- Required by pension authorities
- Life certificate submission

### 6. Government Schemes
- Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT)
- Subsidy crediting
- Scholarship deposits
- Government payment verification

### 7. Legal Document
- Legally valid financial record
- Accepted in courts
- Proof of transactions
- Settlement of disputes

## Types of Bank Accounts

### 1. Savings Account
- For individuals
- Interest-bearing
- Passbook issued
- Minimum balance required

### 2. Current Account
- For businesses
- No interest
- Higher transaction limits
- Statement provided

### 3. Fixed Deposit (FD)
- Fixed amount for fixed period
- Higher interest rates
- Passbook or certificate issued

### 4. Recurring Deposit (RD)
- Regular monthly deposits
- Fixed tenure
- Passbook issued

### 5. Senior Citizen Account
- For citizens above 60 years
- Higher interest rates
- Special benefits

### 6. Minor Account
- For children below 18
- Guardian operated
- Passbook in minor's name

## Contents of Bank Passbook

A typical passbook contains:
- Account holder name
- Account number
- Branch name and code
- IFSC code
- Opening date
- Transaction date
- Particulars (deposit/withdrawal)
- Withdrawal amount
- Deposit amount
- Balance
- Interest credit
- Bank seal and signature

## Modern Alternatives

### E-Passbook
- Electronic version
- Updated in real-time
- Downloadable from bank website/app
- No need to visit branch

### Mobile Banking
- Check balance anytime
- View transactions
- Download statements

### Internet Banking
- Access account online
- Download e-statements
- View transaction history
