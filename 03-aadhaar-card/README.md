# Aadhaar Card

Aadhaar Card is a 12-digit unique identification number issued by UIDAI (Unique Identification Authority of India) that is mandatory for Indians to have for legal and tax compliance in India.

## What is Aadhaar Card?

Aadhaar is a unique 12-digit identification number that serves as:
- **Proof of identity**
- **Proof of address**
- **Demographic and biometric information**

The Aadhaar system is based on:
- **Biometric data**: Fingerprints and iris scans
- **Demographic data**: Name, date of birth, gender, address
- **Unique for life**: One person, one Aadhaar

## Who Issues Aadhaar Cards in India?

**UIDAI (Unique Identification Authority of India)** is the statutory authority established under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology, Government of India, responsible for:
- Issuing Aadhaar numbers
- Managing the Aadhaar database
- Authentication services
- Aadhaar enrollment and updates

## Why You Need an Aadhaar Card

### 1. Public Distribution System (PDS)
Take benefits like food grain under the Public Distribution System

### 2. Banking Services
- Open bank accounts
- KYC compliance
- Eligible to apply for schemes introduced by banks and Government
- Required for loan applications

### 3. Government Data Collection
The Aadhaar card is one of the important documents every Indian citizen needs so the Government can collect and maintain your data efficiently from all over the world

### 4. Financial Transactions
- Buy mutual funds or shares
- Pay income tax
- File ITR (Income Tax Return)
- Link with PAN card (mandatory)

### 5. Government Schemes and Subsidies
- LPG subsidy (PAHAL)
- Scholarship applications
- Pension schemes
- MGNREGA wages
- PM-KISAN benefits
- Ayushman Bharat health insurance

### 6. Mobile SIM Card
Required for purchasing new mobile SIM cards (as per government mandate)

### 7. Digital Services
- Digital locker (DigiLocker)
- E-KYC for online services
- Aadhaar-based authentication

### 8. School Admissions
Many schools require Aadhaar for student enrollment and scholarship applications

## Aadhaar for Children

**Baal Aadhaar (Blue Aadhaar)**:
- For children below 5 years
- No biometric data collected
- Blue-colored Aadhaar card
- Must be updated with biometrics at age 5 and 15

## Unique Features

✅ **12-digit unique number**: No two persons can have the same Aadhaar number
✅ **Paperless**: e-Aadhaar is legally valid
✅ **Biometric-based**: Highly secure and prevents duplication
✅ **Free service**: Enrollment and updates are free
✅ **Portable**: Valid across India
✅ **Voluntary**: Though widely mandatory for services, enrollment is technically voluntary

## Aadhaar Authentication

Aadhaar enables three types of authentication:
1. **Demographic**: Using name, age, gender, address
2. **Biometric**: Using fingerprints or iris scan
3. **OTP-based**: Using mobile number linked to Aadhaar

## Security and Privacy

- Aadhaar data is stored in encrypted format
- UIDAI doesn't share personal information
- Masked Aadhaar available (shows only last 4 digits)
- Virtual ID (VID) can be used instead of Aadhaar number
