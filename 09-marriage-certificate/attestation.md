# Marriage Certificate Attestation

## What is Marriage Certificate Attestation?

Marriage certificate attestation is the process of verifying and authenticating your marriage certificate for use in foreign countries. It is mandatory when you need to prove your marital status abroad for visa applications, spouse immigration, or other legal purposes.

## Why Attestation is Required

- **Visa Applications**: Spouse visa, dependent visa
- **Immigration**: Family reunification, spouse immigration
- **Employment**: Dependent benefits, family sponsorship
- **Property**: Joint property purchase abroad
- **Legal Proceedings**: International legal matters

## Attestation Process

### For Apostille Countries (Hague Convention)

**Step 1: Notary Attestation**
- Get certificate notarized by local notary
- Fee: ₹50-₹200

**Step 2: Home Department/SDM Attestation**
- Submit to State Home Department
- Verification process
- Fee: ₹50-₹100
- Time: 3-5 working days

**Step 3: MEA Apostille**
- Submit to Ministry of External Affairs
- MEA issues Apostille sticker
- Fee: ₹50 per document
- Time: 3-7 working days
- No embassy attestation needed after this

**Total Time**: 10-15 days
**Total Cost**: ₹200-₹400

### For Non-Hague Countries

**Steps 1-2**: Same as above (Notary and Home Department)

**Step 3**: MEA Attestation
- MEA attestation (not Apostille)
- Fee: ₹50

**Step 4**: Embassy Attestation
- Embassy of destination country
- Final attestation
- Fee: ₹500-₹5000 (varies by embassy)
- Time: 5-15 working days

**Total Time**: 20-30 days
**Total Cost**: ₹700-₹6000

## Documents Required for Attestation

1. Original marriage certificate
2. Passport copy (first and last pages)
3. Photographs (passport size)
4. Authorization letter (if using agent)
5. Aadhaar card copy
